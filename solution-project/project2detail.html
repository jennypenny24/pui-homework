masthead app

improving the experience of recovering surgery patients through community building
as the product designer i played a pivotal role in conceptualizing and defining the core features of our mobile application
my role:
product designer
time frame:
february - may, 2023
skills:
prototyping, wireframing, user testing
team:
1 ux designer, 1 ux researcher
problem
masthead is looking to increase trust and develop strong relationships with its customers, as they currently lack a base of loyal customers. simultaneously, customers want a greater sense of community support (professional + patient-to-patients).
goal
how might masthead build trust and foster meaningful relationships with its customers, so the brand can cultivate a base of devoted customers?
research
we conducted comprehensive background research on masthead, utilizing various methods and resources such as unobtrusive online research, analyzing research posters made by our peers, and exploring masthead's website to understand their products and other offerings. our research helped us identify three primary stakeholder groups: patients and caretakers, medical professionals, and masthead.
furthermore, we found opportunities where a product might benefit multiple stakeholder groups. one of the opportunities we identified was a mobile app community platform that could provide support to masthead's customers and their caretakers. we discovered that patients and caretakers were creating their own online support networks, primarily on reddit, while masthead had started to provide support through informational blog posts on their website.
prototyping
we created wireframe sketches using our user journey map. we concluded that a community app would be the most beneficial for customers based on the pain points we identified during our background research. the research revealed that people lacked a designated support community to belong to, making a community app a valuable solution. to validate our designs, we conducted user testing validation sessions with three participants who had undergone major surgery.
we gained a lot of insights through conducting usability tests with stakeholders who were able to point out both flaws and strengths of our prototypes. through this we gained experience in terms of creating low and mid-fi prototypes to validate ideas, conducting surveys with stakeholders to gain feedback, and reflecting on how we can create value through a mobile service.
solution
by connecting the patients with caregivers, medical professionals, and other patients with similar experiences, we believe that the masthead app community could develop to become a safe, secured, and supportive space for customers to build trusting relationships with not just other users but also the company.
