jitter jive

an educational game that aims to help students learn how to manage their performance anxiety
as the lead ux researcher i recruited participants for play testing, conducted various methods of user research, linked insights to design principles, and designed game components to mobilize social interaction.
my role:
ux researcher
time frame:
september - december, 2023
skills:
prototyping, interviewing, user testing
team:
2 ux designers, 1 project manager
problem
performance anxiety (p.a.) is a type of anxiety brought by performing a task, such as a fear of consequences of not being able to perform well. during our research, we found that college students often experience performance anxiety when they are going to take exams, presentations, and/or interviews; the main issue is that many do not know how to manage this p.a. effectively.
goal
how might we help students manage p.a. so that it is less inhibiting?
outcome
designed a cooperative game that teaches players:
· p.a. can help them perform better if they are able to keep it within an optimal range
· common stressors that affect performance quality, as well as some practical coping strategies
research
we discovered through our initial research that social support itself can help people shift their performance anxiety to a more optimal level, but it can also help indirectly to support individuals in the use of other coping strategies. during the design process, we kept this knowledge in mind and heavily integrated it into our game mechanics.
ideation
based on our research, we have confirmed that the direction of our game is collaborative rather than competitive. for the game scenario, we generated extensive ideas with our interviewees such as talent show, singing performance, drama performance, harry potter quidditch team, delivering a new product, driving, etc. because we want to distance the scenario a little bit from the realistic events (exams, interviews, presentations) but make it also accessible for everyone to resonate with, we finally decided to dive into the idea of collaborative school performance.
after brainstorming individually, we fleshed out our ideas as a group on miro and expanded our idea in different dimensions such as learning objectives, settings, nouns, verbs, rules, dynamics, etc.
prototyping
in this phase, we focused more on quality content than presentation. we enriched our design by creating 40+ action cards (including practice cards, relief cards, and motivation cards), 20+ event cards, 5 character cards with different personas and different initial values of performance anxiety and task mastery. we also designed the value changes of each card carefully in order to keep the game balance.
solution
this is a game that people learn from. players not only learn about some p.a. relief and motivating methods that exist, but also the fact that these methods affect each person differently. they learn the methods by reading their action cards, and more so by reading them out loud (improves memory). players learn that these methods affect people differently as different characters are more positively impacted by certain strategies.
reflection
i'm proud to have developed a game that provides a space for people to talk about performance anxiety in a safe and appropriate way. speaking through the lens of their characters, players practice the act of initiating and engaging in conversations about mental health which is something that some struggle with. our role play helps increase empathy for those who may be unlike ourselves and we are happy to see the game teach players the nuances of p.a. regulation.
